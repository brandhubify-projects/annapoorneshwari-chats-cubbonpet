Brand history

A local shop built plate by plate.

Sri Annapoorneshwari Chat earned its reputation through everyday trust: fresh ingredients, fast preparation, affordable plates, and flavours that regulars come back for.

The franchise program is designed to transfer that practical knowledge to serious partners who want to run a food business personally and responsibly.

Instead of treating chaat as only a recipe, the training focuses on repeatable systems: morning prep, counter cleanliness, spice balance, customer handling, event readiness, and daily quality control.

Best fit: owner-operators who can be present, learn hands-on, respect hygiene, and care about consistent taste every day.

Franchise process

A simple path from enquiry to launch readiness.

The exact commercial model can be discussed directly. This page sets the expectation for how a serious enquiry can move forward.

Initial enquiry

Send your city, contact details, food business background, and why you want to open a chaat outlet.

Conversation

The team speaks with you about location, investment readiness, operating involvement, and expected timeline.

Training plan

Shortlisted partners learn recipes, prep flow, hygiene discipline, serving style, sourcing basics, and launch menu planning.

Launch support

Guidance on counter setup, opening menu, early customer feedback, event enquiries, and maintaining consistent taste.

How we teach

Hands-on learning, not just a recipe sheet.

Chaat is a timing business. The taste depends on preparation, freshness, proportion, and the confidence of the person behind the counter.

Counter training

Learn how to assemble plates quickly, keep the service area clean, handle rush periods, and serve with the same warmth customers expect from the Cubbonpet shop.

Menu discipline

Understand which items to launch first, how to keep ingredients fresh, and how to build a compact menu that is practical for daily operations.

Taste calibration

Practice sweet, spicy, tangy, crunchy, and creamy balance so popular plates stay recognisable and consistent.

Business readiness

Discuss location fit, staffing, basic equipment, sourcing, price positioning, local marketing, and event order handling.
